# Evidence-First Co-Decision

**Status:** GOVERNING. Defines the joint decision protocol between Midas and the user. Anchored to FP-8 (evidence-first co-decision) and the owner's turn-3 framing: _"compute and find the evidence to support your recommendations and we decide on the action together."_

---

## 1. Principle

> **Midas assembles the evidence. The user weighs it. The decision is ours.**

This is neither "AI decides" nor "human decides." It is a joint protocol in which:

1. Midas produces a recommendation together with the evidence that supports it, its uncertainty, its counterfactuals, and the precise threshold that would flip the call.
2. The user reads, challenges, modifies, or approves — and the Debate surface provides tools that let them _actually_ change the recommendation, not just argue about it.
3. The final decision is recorded with provenance: who contributed what, and under what evidence.

---

## 2. The Brief Contract

Every recommendation ships with a brief that contains the following sections. The brief is generated by a frontier LLM (FP-12) from the evidence store. Missing sections are bugs.

### 2.1 Thesis

The core claim in one sentence. Not "reduce NVDA by 15%" — that is the action. The thesis is the causal sentence: _"Implied vol has risen ahead of earnings; the current position is 2× target weight; the expected shortfall at the current `z_t` exceeds the envelope tail budget."_

### 2.2 Evidence

A structured list of the data points the model used, with their uncertainty:

- `z_t` projection relevant to this decision (factor-basis rendering for human readability)
- Head outputs with posteriors
- Recent calibration of those heads in similar `z_t` regions
- Historical analogues (from `06-continuous-regime-rendering.md` §6)
- News / research context from the RAG

Every evidence item carries a **provenance pointer**: a fabric row, a model version, a tool call, a document ID. The Debate agent can drill into any item.

### 2.3 If Approved

The consequence of taking the action. Concretely:

- New allocation after the trade
- Estimated transaction costs (spreads, slippage, commissions, dividend withholding implications per FP-6)
- Expected portfolio risk metrics after the move (vol, VaR, tail)
- Expected drawdown profile under the current `z_t` posterior

### 2.4 If Rejected

The consequence of not taking the action:

- Current position retained
- Expected drawdown profile under the current `z_t` posterior
- Pool disagreement on the rejection (is the rejection aligned with any challenger?)
- How long the decision window extends if the user defers

### 2.5 Historical Precedent

Comparable past decisions and their outcomes. Not cherry-picked — the top K analogues by state similarity. Each precedent links to the decision, the debate (if any), the outcome, and the counterfactual that was tracked.

### 2.6 What Would Change My Mind (Mandatory)

The single most important section, and one that did not exist in Phase 01 briefs. It names the specific evidence or threshold that would flip Midas's recommendation:

> _"I would move from 'reduce 15%' to 'hold' if implied vol contracted below X, or if tonight's policy statement moved the yield curve above Y, or if the sector-CNN's confidence fell below Z. You should watch these."_

This section is the forensic trail. It forces the model to commit to a hypothesis in terms the user can verify. A brief without this section is a brief without a contract.

### 2.7 Confidence

Not a single number. The confidence is a posterior over the recommendation's expected utility, accompanied by the specific factors driving it (narrow posterior on `z_t`, calibration on this head, precedent strength, pool agreement).

## 3. The Debate Protocol

### 3.1 Entry Points

The user can enter the Debate surface from anywhere: a pending decision, a historical decision, a position, a news item, the backtest, or the Debate tab directly. Context is pre-loaded.

### 3.2 Debate Is A Joint Evidence Review, Not Rhetoric

The Debate agent's job is not to win an argument. It is to:

- Surface evidence the user has not yet seen
- Present counter-evidence when the user's argument is incomplete
- Re-run the optimizer under user-proposed constraints
- Generate counterfactuals on demand
- Update the pending decision when the evidence warrants
- Concede when the user makes a stronger case

### 3.3 Tools The Debate Agent Must Have

> A Debate agent without these tools is a chatbot on a PDF — and FP-8 explicitly forbids that.

| Tool                                                 | What it does                                                        | Why it matters                                                                       |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| `query_fabric(query)`                                | Reads any data in the fabric                                        | Every claim in a debate is grounded                                                  |
| `query_head(head_id, context)`                       | Invokes a specific model head                                       | The user can ask "what does the cross-sectional GNN say?"                            |
| `query_calibration(head_id, z_region)`               | Returns calibration data for a head in a latent region              | "How often is your momentum head right in states like this?"                         |
| `retrieve_analogue(z_t)`                             | Fetches historical analogues                                        | Evidence from past                                                                   |
| `propose_alternative_allocation(constraints)`        | Re-runs the optimizer                                               | User: "what if the max position was 8%?"                                             |
| `recompute_with_constraint(decision_id, constraint)` | Applies a user constraint to a pending decision                     | User: "ignore the momentum signal for this one"                                      |
| `backtest_scenario(scenario)`                        | Runs a lightweight scenario backtest                                | User: "what would this policy have done in 2018?"                                    |
| `update_decision(decision_id, patch)`                | Actually writes to the pending decision                             | **This tool is what makes Debate not a chatbot.**                                    |
| `generate_counterfactual(decision_id, alternative)`  | Computes what would have happened if an alternative had been chosen | Feeds the learning loop (FP-14)                                                      |
| `surface_override_pattern()`                         | Reports the user's recent override patterns and their outcomes      | Lets the agent say: "you overrode three similar decisions; those holds have cost X." |

### 3.4 Confidence And Uncertainty In Debate

When the agent presents evidence, it names its own uncertainty. When the user asks a question the agent cannot answer confidently, the agent says so:

> _"The current state is 0.31 on the cross-asset similarity axis from the closest training exemplar — that's near the edge of what I've been calibrated on. I can give you the policy head's recommendation but I am telling you the calibration is thinner here than in most states."_

No agent output ever claims confidence the underlying model does not have. Calibration is always available to be quoted.

### 3.5 Resolution States

A debate thread resolves into one of four states. The user's Q3 answer allowed Debate to end without necessarily closing a decision — the attention budget matters. Resolution states:

| State                        | Meaning                                                                                                                                  |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Decision updated**         | The recommendation has been mutated (via `update_decision`); user proceeds to approve/reject the new version                             |
| **Decision maintained**      | The user read the evidence and the recommendation stands; user returns to the Decisions surface                                          |
| **Open / thinking out loud** | Allowed by the owner's turn-3 answer — Debate can end without changing a pending decision; the thread is preserved for resumption        |
| **Envelope change proposed** | Debate surfaced that the decision implicates the trust boundary; user is routed to envelope adjustment (which requires its own approval) |

### 3.6 Thread Memory

Debate threads persist across sessions. A user resuming a thread three days later sees the full context. The agent has access to the thread history plus everything in the evidence store. When a prior debate became a new decision, the new decision links back to the thread.

## 4. Evidence-First In Practice: A Walkthrough

**Context:** Tactical head proposes reducing equity exposure by 8% based on rising tail risk in the `z_t` posterior.

**Brief generated:**

- _Thesis:_ "Tail-head posterior has widened over the last 5 sessions; current expected shortfall is within envelope but moving toward the limit."
- _Evidence:_ `z_t` shows higher distance-from-training + lower model agreement; tail head calibration in this region is 0.78; cross-sectional GNN confidence is moderate; no high-weight news item in the last 24 hours.
- _If approved:_ costs $X, brings expected shortfall to Y, reduces upside capture by Z.
- _If rejected:_ current shortfall trajectory stays on current path; estimated envelope breach probability 12% over next 10 days.
- _Precedent:_ three analogues in the last 10 years; in two, the system reduced and avoided drawdown; in one, the rejection outperformed by 1.2%.
- _What would change my mind:_ "If model agreement recovered to 0.8 or higher, or if the tail-head posterior narrowed, I would move this recommendation to 'hold'."
- _Confidence:_ mean 0.66, posterior width 0.12.

**User debates:**

_"Why should I trust the tail head here? I remember it was wrong in March."_

Agent calls `query_calibration(tail_head, current_z_t)`, compares to March-analogue calibration, responds:

_"The tail head's calibration in the March analogue was 0.52 — you're right that it underperformed there. In the current state it is at 0.78, which is above the floor, but thinner than its best regions. Let me show you the calibration curve. Also — the pool is not unanimous on this one. Here is what the classical risk-parity challenger proposes: a 3% reduction instead of 8%."_

User: _"Propose 3% reduction and show me."_

Agent calls `recompute_with_constraint(decision_id, max_reduction=3%)`. New brief shows updated consequences. User approves the modified version with biometric.

The thread is preserved with the user's override pattern feeding into future priors.

## 5. Override Learning

Every user override is a signal. The counterfactual of overridden decisions is tracked in the `decisions` table (at 1d / 1w / 1m horizons) and feeds:

- The user's visible override-pattern dashboard
- The Debate agent's priors when similar decisions recur
- The autonomy ladder's degradation or upgrade signal (FP-14)

**Important:** override outcomes are not used to auto-retrain models. Retraining is governed by the outer loop of `05-model-pool-and-meta-router.md` and has its own evidence contract. Overrides inform the _router_ (contextual bandit context) and the _user-facing debate_, not the underlying model weights directly.

## 6. Confidence Under The Attention Budget

The owner's Q3 answer: _"humans have cognitive load, you need to work with the AI UI UX specialists to ensure that my attention is well spent."_

Applied to briefs:

- A routine approval (low `a_t`, Calm band, small dollar impact, high confidence) ships with a **compressed brief** — one-sentence thesis, one key number, the "what would change my mind" line, a tap-to-expand for the full evidence
- An Urgent approval (high `a_t`, large dollar impact, narrow window) ships with the **full structured brief** plus a summary card pinned to the top
- A Crisis state (out-of-distribution `z_t`) ships with the full brief + an explicit "I am less calibrated in this state" honesty banner

The compression function is tied to decision weight, not surface bandwidth. The brief templates are a matrix over `(a_t band, dollar-impact tier, confidence tier)`, defined in `09-surfaces-and-attention.md`.

## 7. Audit And Provenance

Every decision writes an immutable record with:

- The full brief as generated
- The pool members consulted + their outputs
- The router's blending decision
- The compliance-agent checks that passed
- The user's action (approve / modify / reject / debate)
- Any debate thread that informed the decision
- The execution result (fills, costs, reconciled quantities)
- The counterfactual outcome computed after the horizon closes

This record is the substrate for:

- Track record scoring (`12-performance-and-track-record.md`)
- Brinson attribution
- Promotion/demotion contracts (`05-model-pool-and-meta-router.md`)
- Debate over past decisions
- Regulatory audit if the system is ever commercialized

---

## 8. What This Commits Us To

- Every recommendation ships a brief with all seven sections; missing sections are bugs
- The Debate agent has tools that write to pending decisions; a read-only debate is not a debate
- Frontier LLMs generate every brief and drive every debate (FP-12)
- Confidence is a distribution, not a number, at every layer from model output to brief
- Calibration is always quotable; the agent can always justify confidence with data
- Override patterns inform the router and the user-facing debate, not direct model-weight updates
- Brief density is a function of decision weight, not a uniform template
- Every decision has immutable audit provenance
