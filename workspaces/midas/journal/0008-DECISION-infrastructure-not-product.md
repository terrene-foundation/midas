## Decision

Midas is not competing against Wealthfront, Betterment, or any robo-advisory service. It IS the entire robo-advisory capability — back office, middle office, front office — put into the user's hands for free. The tool replaces the need for any advisory service.

## Alternatives Considered

1. **Robo-advisor competitor** — Frame Midas as "better than Wealthfront." Rejected by user: "This is a totally different value proposition."
2. **Augmented advisory** — Frame as helping existing advisors. Not the intent.
3. **Democratized infrastructure** — Chosen. Midas gives individuals the same portfolio management capability as institutional desks.

## Rationale

User stated directly: "This tool in the hands of a user puts the entire roboadvisory, Wealthfront etc. into their hands FOR FREE. We are not managing users funds, we are creating a tool that replaces the entire back, middle, and front office."

This fundamentally changes the framing from "what does Wealthfront lack?" to "what does an institutional portfolio desk have that individuals don't?" The competitive analysis against robo-advisors in the initial value audit was the wrong frame entirely.

## Consequences

- All competitive analysis should be reframed: understand institutional capabilities to democratize, not retail products to beat
- Value proposition language shifts from "better than X" to "you no longer need X"
- Commercialization path may be open-source infrastructure, not managed service
- The product's sophistication ceiling is raised — if Goldman's desk can do it, Midas should aspire to it

## For Discussion

- Does the "democratized institutional infrastructure" framing extend to ALL institutional capabilities, or are there specific capabilities that are impractical to democratize (e.g., prime brokerage relationships, dark pool access, OTC derivatives)?
- If the long-term vision is open-source institutional-grade infrastructure, what is the sustainable development model? (Foundation-funded, community-driven, or commercial product built on open infrastructure?)
