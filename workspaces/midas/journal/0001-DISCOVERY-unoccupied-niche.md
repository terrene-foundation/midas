## What Was Discovered

Market research across robo-advisors ($500B+ combined AUM), algorithmic platforms (QuantConnect, Alpaca), and LLM-era entrants (Composer, RockFlow, Kavout) confirmed that no existing product combines all of:

1. Autonomous execution with regime-aware strategy rotation
2. Conversational AI that debates investment decisions with the user
3. Multi-asset class coverage (ETFs, bonds, REITs, commodities, precious metals, EM)
4. Professional broker integration (IBKR)
5. Transparent cost modeling (slippage, price impact)
6. Multi-horizon backtesting with regime-specific validation

Composer comes closest but is equity-only, subscription-based, and lacks regime awareness. The AI debate capability is entirely unoccupied — a 2025 University of Minnesota study found 78% of SEC-registered robo-advisors lack any explainability.

## Why It Matters

This validates the product concept as genuinely differentiated, not just "another robo-advisor with AI." The AI debate feature specifically occupies an uncontested niche. The risk is not competition — it is execution complexity from trying to do all six things at once.

## Follow-Up

- Prioritize the AI debate feature as the primary differentiator (not just a checkbox feature)
- Consider launching with fewer asset classes to reduce initial complexity, then expand
- Track Composer and RockFlow for convergence toward Midas's feature set

## For Discussion

- If Composer adds regime detection and conversational AI (plausible within 12 months), what remains defensible about Midas's position?
- The 78% explainability gap among robo-advisors — is this because users don't demand it, or because it's technically hard? User research data on demand for AI explainability in finance would strengthen or weaken this thesis.
- Would launching with a single differentiator (AI debate only, no autonomous execution) be a faster path to market while the full system is built?
